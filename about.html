About Me

My name is Hallie King, and for the past three years, I have been fortunate enough to study Communication Design at the Sam Fox School of Design & Visual Arts at Washington University in St. Louis. Additionally, I have been able to broaden my design skillset through a minor in Human-Computer Interaction.

In my design work, the main driving force of creativity often comes from diving deep into the research phase of an assignment. In past projects, I have been inspired by topics including (but not limited to) astronomy, the history of women in comic design, the major arcana, tango dancing, ancient Greek pottery, Nobel laureate Rita Levi-Montalcini, one specific blue whale, classic literature, musical theater, and Los Angeles street art. I consider it a privilege to explore niche topics and to share them in a new light through design work that crosses multiple disciplines.

When I’m not designing I can often still be found making something, whether it’s a knitting or crochet project, a dessert (I make a great peach pie), or one of many miniature gouache paintings.

Reach out any time at hallieking525@gmail.com or hallie@wustl.edu.
